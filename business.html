Business Model

Start manual, prove trust, then automate only what real customers use.

Launch strategy

  1. Start with one or two trusted runners.
  2. Take limited customers by referral or partner introduction.
  3. Run requests manually through a simple form and text updates.
  4. Track time, miles, wait time, parking, customer issues, and runner satisfaction.
  5. Build the app after patterns are proven.

Best first partners

Dry cleaners, pharmacies, travel agencies, immigration attorneys, high-rise buildings, law offices, accountants, real estate offices, and senior communities.

Why partnerships matter

A new app has no trust. A known business already has trust. By partnering with companies customers already use, the service borrows credibility and gets warmer introductions.

Revenue streams

Route fees

First stop, additional stops, long-distance route quotes.

Beta usage fee

Gradual membership-style fee collected per completed request, capped monthly if desired.

Priority fees

Higher pricing for faster windows and scarce runner availability.

Mission-critical fees

Airport rescue, passport retrieval, medication, legal documents, urgent business materials.

Administrative services

Passport office, DMV, embassy, permits, paperwork drop-offs and pickups.

Business accounts

Recurring work from offices and buildings that need reliable runners.

Positioning

Not the cheapest. Not the fastest in every case. The most transparent and reliable local helper when the job matters.
